# 8 · Interview Practice

**TL;DR:** Staff interviews test whether you can frame ambiguity, make a decision, defend
trade-offs and lead the system after launch. Rehearse answers aloud, not silently.

## Likely interview surfaces

1. **Biometric depth:** embeddings, losses, thresholds, protocols and failure analysis.
2. **Fairness:** measurement, uncertainty, mitigation and responsible communication.
3. **Security:** PAD, deepfakes, injection and adaptive attack evaluation.
4. **System design:** global training-to-serving architecture on AWS.
5. **Coding:** clean Python/NumPy/OpenCV/PyTorch with tests and edge cases.
6. **Leadership:** mentoring, review quality, cross-team decisions and incident ownership.

## Answer timing

- **30 seconds:** definition plus most important trade-off.
- **2 minutes:** structured approach and concrete decision.
- **10–15 minutes:** requirements, diagram, APIs/data, scale, failure, security,
  fairness, monitoring and alternatives.

## Behavioral preparation

Prepare five STAR stories:

- improved a model/system using evidence rather than opinion;
- production incident you owned through prevention;
- design disagreement resolved with measurable criteria;
- engineer you mentored into greater ownership;
- project where you reduced scope or said no to protect quality/security.

At Staff level, emphasize how you changed the **system and team**, not only your personal
implementation.
